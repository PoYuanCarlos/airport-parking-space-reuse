Problem Context

Why this project matters

Airports depend on parking for passenger access, operational reliability, and non-aeronautical revenue. As shared autonomous mobility grows, long-term parking demand may decline while curbside pickup/drop-off, staging, charging, and flexible access-management needs may increase.

This project frames the issue as an airport access and facility-reuse decision problem, not only a parking-demand forecast.

Core Question

How can airports evaluate the operational, financial, spatial, and stakeholder impacts of shared autonomous mobility on parking demand, curbside pressure, and future parking-space reuse under uncertain adoption scenarios?

Decision Logic

Data → Insight → Decision → Recommendation

Data Insight Decision Recommendation
Long-term parking demand by scenario High shared-mobility adoption may reduce parking utilization. Avoid overbuilding fixed parking capacity. Delay expansion and prepare flexible reuse options.
Curbside and staging demand proxies Parking decline may shift pressure to pickup/drop-off zones. Reallocate selected areas for managed shared-mobility operations. Pilot geofenced pickup, staging, and turnover controls.
Reuse-option scorecard Different reuse options perform differently by scenario. Select options based on flexibility, revenue, operations, and equity. Use a decision matrix rather than one universal recommendation.
